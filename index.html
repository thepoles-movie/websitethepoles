Logline: The Poles

NYC, 1983: Two strip-club owning thugs hire a playwright to create a play for their strippers that will stop the city from closing their club, but the thugs turn murderous, and the playwright must run for his life.

Genre: Drama / Thriller / Comedy/ Noir ... A story based on actual events.

Synopsis: The Poles

It’s 1983 in filthy, urine-drenched New York City, where 24 year old New Wave film, music, and Pop Art aficionado Don Asdale is hustling to make it as a playwright. He's hit hard times - his live-in girlfriend left him, his apartment's in shambles, and finding a job in his chosen profession? Next to impossible.

Don reads an ad in the Village Voice newspaper - an open call for an actual, paying, playwriting gig. But he doesn't have the main requirement - playwriting awards. That doesn't stop him; he counterfeits his own award certificates.

To Don's amazement, the job is writing a play for a Times Square strip club. To keep their club from being shut down by the city's new liquor licensing laws - meant to clean up Times Square - management plans to legitimize their club by turning it into a faux "theater" with a "play", to be written by a proven (with awards), established playwright. Management is Franco and Nicky, two felons epitomizing strip club sleaze. They take a liking to Don, and offer him the job. After much soul searching, Don accepts - giving his name and sham accomplishments to Franco for marquee billing on the new "theater".

Don starts rehearsals at the club, meeting the ultimate Pole dancer, Nu-wave goddess Capri. They hit it off immediately, becoming more involved while he readied his play for opening night at the club. But just ten minutes into the premier, Franco - infuriated that the play does not include any of the nudity and filth he requested - chases Don out of the club with threats to kill him.

When Don flees to Pennsylvania to hide out while things calm down, Franco forces Capri to lure Don back to town with a fake murder/blackmail story. As Franco is set to kill him on an East Village rooftop, Don attempts to see through the half-truths and lies, trying to figure out who, if anyone, is on his side.
